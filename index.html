Data in Health - Africa Program 2024 (DIHAP24) (BETA)

The Data in Health - Africa Program 2024 (DIHAP24) is a collaborative capacity building workshop by the African Development Bank (AfDB) through its Data Innovation Lab (DIL), and the World Bank Development Economics Institute (DEC). This workshop aims to enhance the data-driven decision-making capabilities of technical staff from National Statistical Offices (NSOs) and Ministry of Health (MoH)/health agencies across 10-12 African countries. This endeavor is part of the ongoing joint AfDB-BMGF project, which includes a comprehensive data collection exercise on healthcare facilities across the 12 project-involved countries.

Expected Outcomes

SKILLED WORKFORCE

A cadre of technical staff from NSOs and MoH/health agencies, proficient in applying modern data science techniques to health data. The workshop presents a valuable opportunity for teams engaged in healthcare facilities data collection as part of the AfDB-BMGF joint project, fostering collaborative efforts and shared learning.

DATA-DRIVEN HEALTH POLICIES

Enhanced capacity of African countries to formulate and implement health policies and interventions based on robust data analysis and sentiment tracking.

ADVANCED DATA SOLUTIONS

Implementation of advanced big data solutions for assessing health service accessibility, particularly demonstrated through the Kigali health facilities prototype.

ENHANCED DATA UTILIZATION

Improved capability to apply collected data in evidence-based decision-making processes. This will enable the development of more precise and impactful health interventions, as illustrated through data use scenarios and applications that will be showcased during the workshop.

PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT

Improved understanding and incorporation of public perceptions and concerns in health policies and initiatives, informed by sentiment analysis and social media monitoring.
